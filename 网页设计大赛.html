History

The city name of Glasgow derives from the "glas Cu" in the Spanish language, which means "green open space"". Modern and contemporary time

At the beginning of the sixteenth Century, Glasgow was an important religious and academic city. The deepening of the Clyde River in 1770s promoted the development of Glasgow industry, especially shipbuilding industry in nineteenth Century.

Now

In twentieth Century, Glasgow's economy began to decline because of the great depression and the two World War in 1930s. In the middle of the 1980s, many financial companies moved in, and Glasgow's economy improved significantly.
